## Framing for the ask

Do NOT say "we're building a tool and want feedback." That primes them to talk about the tool.

Say: "I'm trying to understand how AMs think about cross-sell on their Tables accounts. Specifically xtraCHEF - it keeps coming up as an underutilized product. 20 minutes, no prep needed."

---

## Questions

**Warm up (2 min)**
- How long have you been managing Tables accounts?
- Roughly how many Tables accounts are you carrying right now?

**Core questions (12 min)**

1. "Walk me through what you know about xtraCHEF. What does it actually do for a restaurant?"
   - Listen for: depth of product knowledge, can they articulate the value prop without reading from a script, do they know the COGS reduction frame?

2. "When you're in a call with a Tables customer - how often does xtraCHEF come up?"
   - Listen for: "almost never" vs. "I try to bring it up" vs. "only when they ask"

3. "When you don't bring it up - what's going through your head?"
   - This is the key question. Do NOT prompt. Let them answer.
   - Listen for: "I'm not confident enough to pitch it," "I don't know what to say when they push back," "my quota doesn't reward it," "it doesn't fit most of my accounts," "I focus on Tables renewal first"

4. "Has a Tables customer ever pushed back on xtraCHEF? What did they say?"
   - Listen for: specific objections they've encountered. If they can't recall any, they haven't been pitching it.

5. "If you had a tool that showed you exactly which of your Tables accounts don't have xtraCHEF, and how your attach rate compares to your peers - would that change what you do?"
   - Listen for: "yes, I'd know where to start" (knowledge/prioritization gap) vs. "I already know which ones, I just don't pursue them" (choice/incentive gap)

**Close (3 min)**

6. "What's the one thing that would make you more likely to pitch xtraCHEF on your next Tables call?"
   - Open-ended. Don't suggest answers.

7. "Is there anyone else on your team who has figured this out - someone whose xtraCHEF attach is notably higher than average?"
   - This surfaces top-performer candidates for Build 2 (Chorus pull).

Record in `docs/INTERVIEW_FINDINGS.md`:

| Question | Answer summary | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Product knowledge depth | 1-5 (1=can't explain it, 5=knows specific objection counters) | |
| Pitch frequency | Never / Rarely / Sometimes / Often | |
| Primary reason for not pitching | [record verbatim] | |
| Would the panel change behavior? | Yes / Maybe / No | |
| Top performer name given | [name] | |

**Diagnosis rubric:**

- Knowledge score 1-2 + "not confident" = **knowledge gap** → Build 4 is right
- Knowledge score 3-5 + "quota/time/fit" = **choice/incentive gap** → different intervention
- Knowledge score 3-5 + "don't know where to start" = **prioritization gap** → Build 1 CTA is right
- Mixed (2 knowledge, 3 choice) = **both** → Build 1+4, but sequence matters

## What to do with findings

Document in `docs/INTERVIEW_FINDINGS.md` before Aug 15.

If 3+ of 5 say knowledge gap: proceed to Build 3 + 4 as planned.
If 3+ of 5 say choice: stop after Build 1. The panel is still useful for prioritization but the product knowledge layer (Build 4) won't move attach rates. Bring the finding to Craig before Oct 1.
If mixed: pilot Build 3 with the knowledge-gap AMs only, instrument separately.
